# Healer and Destroyer

Build the ship. Carve the world. Keep your allies alive. Blow everything else apart.

**Healer and Destroyer** is a 2D web-based multiplayer cave-exploration action game where players pilot modular ships through destructible underground worlds, mine resources from the terrain itself, construct better hulls, battle hostile machines, and choose whether to become a frontline destroyer, a battlefield healer, or something dangerous in between.

This project is built around a simple but powerful fantasy:

- explore deeper
- build smarter
- survive longer
- fight harder
- keep going forever

## Why This Project Is Exciting

This is not just another top-down shooter.

Healer and Destroyer combines:

- **Destructible cave worlds**: every block can be broken, mined, or fought through
- **Modular ship design**: hulls, hardpoints, weapons, engines, support gear, and expansion paths all matter
- **PvE and PvP from day one**: explore solo, form teams, or turn every tunnel into a war zone
- **Persistent worlds**: your resources, ships, and discovered map network survive across sessions
- **Effectively infinite exploration**: finite cave maps connect into a growing procedural world graph
- **Distinct playstyles**: evolve from a simple scout into a healer, destroyer, miner, or massive multi-hull machine

## Core Fantasy

You begin with a tiny ship and a forward laser.

From there, the world opens up.

Mine rock and ore. Build better hulls. Hunt enemy foundries. Defend your structures. Discover new cave networks through hidden exits and portals. Recover salvage after disaster. Grow a stable of ships. Return to the same persistent world and push deeper into the unknown.

Every choice changes how you survive:

- more firepower
- more support
- more mobility
- more engineering
- more ambition

## Current Direction

The design currently centers on:

- a browser-based client with a server-authoritative simulation
- long-lived persistent worlds that pause when no players are online
- finite persistent maps connected into an open-ended procedural network
- ship builder sites for construction and ship management
- instant module crafting, timed ship construction, and fast ship swaps at builder sites
- random enemy presence plus foundry-driven enemy production
